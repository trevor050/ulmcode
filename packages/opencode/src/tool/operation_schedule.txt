Create a durable ULMCode operation graph and lane schedule.

Use this after `operation_plan` and before launching broad execution. It converts the operation into required parallel lanes with dependencies, model routes, budgets, allowed tools, expected artifacts, safety mode, and stale recovery policy. The graph is the resumable work queue for long-running operations; do not rely on prose-only plans for overnight execution.

For long and overnight operations, the schedule must account for both orchestrator and supervisor responsibilities. If the generated graph does not yet have explicit `orchestrator` and `supervisor` lanes, the plan must still name how `operation_supervise` will be invoked on startup, heartbeat, post-compaction, and pre-handoff reviews. A supervisor review can block launch, request recovery, queue validation, or require report/runtime fixes before handoff.

Do not schedule long foreground commands. Command lanes expected to exceed two minutes must be launched through `command_supervise` or background `task` lanes and reconciled through operation metadata.
